Question 1: Tell us about a cool AI project you have worked on?

One of the projects I'm genuinely proud of is VendorDesk — an AI-powered quotation generator I built for small vendors, traders, and wholesalers, primarily in India.

The problem was pretty clear once I spent time talking to a few traders: these folks run their entire business on WhatsApp. A customer messages them asking for a quote, and they either reply with a rough text message or scramble to make a PDF manually — sometimes spending 15–20 minutes on a single quotation. For a vendor juggling 30–40 customers a day, that's a huge time sink, and the output still looks unprofessional.

My approach was to build a mobile app that lets a vendor describe an order the way they'd naturally say it — in English, Hindi, or Hinglish — and have AI do the heavy lifting. So instead of filling out a form line by line, a vendor can say or type something like "50 kg basmati, 2 bags cement, 10 litre sarso oil" and the app extracts each line item, matches it against their product catalogue, pulls in the right prices and units, and generates a clean, branded PDF quotation — ready to share on WhatsApp in under a minute.

I also added a voice-to-quotation flow using multimodal AI, so vendors who aren't comfortable typing can just record a voice note of the customer's order. On top of that, I built a catalogue extraction feature where vendors can photograph their physical rate card or price list and the app automatically populates their product catalogue — which removed a big onboarding hurdle.

The app works offline-first since connectivity in smaller towns can be unreliable, so everything saves locally and syncs to the cloud in the background when the connection is restored.

The impact has been meaningful — vendors who tested it reported cutting their quotation time from 15+ minutes down to under 60 seconds, and customers responded better to receiving a proper PDF versus a WhatsApp text. It also gave vendors a simple CRM-like view of their leads and quote status, which most of them never had before.

Question 2: Tools & Technologies Used?

Here's the stack I worked with across the AI and product layers:

LLMs & AI:
- Google Gemini 2.5 Flash — used for natural language understanding, structured data extraction from free-form text and voice, multilingual support (English, Hindi, Hinglish), and catalogue extraction from images/files
- Google Generative AI SDK — for integrating Gemini into the backend orchestration layer

Backend & Orchestration:
- Node.js with Express — AI orchestration proxy that sits between the mobile app and the Gemini API, handling prompt construction, response parsing, and validation
- Zod — schema validation for structured AI outputs to ensure reliability

Database & Storage:
- Supabase (PostgreSQL) — primary cloud database, authentication, and file storage
- AsyncStorage — local persistence for offline-first functionality
- Custom FIFO sync queue with mutex-based background sync — to handle offline-to-cloud synchronization reliably

Mobile Frontend:
- React Native with Expo (SDK 54+) and TypeScript
- Expo Router for navigation
- React Context for state management
- expo-print for HTML-to-PDF generation
- expo-sharing for native file sharing
- expo-av for audio recording (voice input)

Infrastructure & Tooling:
- pnpm monorepo — shared libraries for API clients and database schemas across mobile and backend
- Supabase Auth — OTP-based authentication
